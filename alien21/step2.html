The first thing we need to do is give ourselves a nice, big alien head. For this we will need the Liquify filter.

  1. Duplicate the original layer (which should be named Background) by right-clicking the layer name and choosing Duplicate Layer

We will need the original layer on Step 6.

  1. Rename the new layer to Alien (it should be at the top of the layer stack)
  2. Make sure the Alien layer is selected
  3. Click Filter and then click Liquify to open the Liquify window
  4. If you can't see all of your image in the Liquify window, roll the mouse wheel down until you can see your entire head
  5. Select the Blow Tool on the left side of the Liquify window...
  6. On the right side, set Rate to 80...

Rate controls the speed at which our face is distorted by the Liquify tools - the higher the number the quicker the distortion. Since we are new to the Liquify tools, 80 is a good amount to use as it will prevent Photopea from making changes too fast, but won't go so slow that we get frustrated.

  1. Press the ] key on the keyboard repeatedly to increase the brush size until your brush circle is just large enough to fit your head...

    Note that pressing and holding the ] key will cause the size of the brush to increase gradually, while pressing the [ key on the keyboard will decrease the brush size
  2. Click in the middle of your forehead and hold down the left mouse button to grow your head...

    Don't make it too large or it will just look ridiculous
  3. Now click on the left temple and grow your head some more...
  4. Now repeat the process on the right temple...

Don't worry if you can't get your head to look exactly right or if it doesn't look as good as mine. This is your first time using this tool, and it may take a while for you to be really proficient with it. Remember that our goal here is to be introduced to this tool and what it can do, not to become an expert with it overnight. Just get your head to a nice shape you can live with and move on.

Aliens usually have tiny mouths and chins, so let's use another feature of the Liquify window to reduce the size of the chin.
  1. Click the Shrink Tool...

    Note that the Shrink Tool is the opposite of the Blow Tool
  2. Decrease the size of your brush (remember the keyboard shortcut to change the size of the brush? Hope so cause I'm not going to tell you here) so that it covers just your face (eyes, nose, mouth, and chin)...

A quick hint: in reality, what size you make your brush at this point is up to you. Just choose a size and then do the following steps and determine if you like the results or not. If you don't, remember that you can always use Ctrl+Z or go into the History panel to undo the shrink then change the size of your brush and reshrink your face until you get something that you like.
  1. Place the center of the circle on your chin
  2. Press and hold the left mouse button until you have a nicely shaped tiny alien chin...
  3. Decrease the size of your brush slightly (again, exactly what size you make it is up to you) and repeat the shrink process on your ears, nose, and mouth - if you can't see enough of your ears to be able to work with them then just ignore them...

Our next job is turn our thin human eyes into large alien eyes. We will return to the Blow Tool for this.
  1. Select the Blow Tool
  2. Adjust the size of your brush so that it is just wide enough to cover both eyes
  3. Click in the middle of your left eye to enlarge it - don't go crazy with your size, look at the image below and the examples at the end of Step 1 to get an idea of a good eye size
  4. Still using your left eye, click and enlarge the left side of the eye
  5. Still using your left eye, click and enlarge the right side of the eye
  6. Repeat the process to enlarge your right eye - you should end up with nice, large alien eyes...

If your eyes do not have the large alien look they should and instead look rather flattened, it is likely that you were squinting or that the natural shape of your eyes just doesn't work well with the above process (this is fine - not everyone's face works well as an alien). Try further shaping your eye by reducing the size of your brush so that it is just wide enough to cover one eye then click and enlarge in the middle of each eye - this should give you the nice bulging, alien eyes we are looking for (but again, don't go crazy or it will just look bad). Don't worry too much about how the pupil or sclera looks at this point as we will be adjusting the coloring of the eyes in Step 4.

You should be looking pretty alien by now, so we only need a few simple adjustments to complete the head. Let's give the mouth a nice smile. For this we will use the Smudge tool. This tool allows us to click and drag areas of our image to reposition them. For example, we can drag the very tip of our chin down, or give ourselves pointed ears, or make any other similar small adjustment.
  1. Select the Smudge Tool...
  2. Change the brush size so that it is just large enough to cover the mouth then click and drag the corners of the mouth up
  3. Now increase the size of the brush slightly and drag the tip of the chin down just a little
  4. Now decrease the size of the brush and give your subject pointed ears (if you can)
  5. Use the Smudge Tool to make any other adjustments you would like to make (don't go crazy with it!) - your character should now have a distinct alien look…

Be careful not to go crazy with the Smudge Tool. While it is a great tool to make specific adjustments to make your alien head look unique, if overused it becomes obvious that we are manipulating our image with Photopea.

  1. Click OK on the right side to exit the Liquify window
  2. Save your work in your Alien folder

Now that we have an alien head, let's turn ourselves green.
